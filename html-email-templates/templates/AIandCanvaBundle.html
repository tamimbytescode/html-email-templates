AI Action Summit 2025: A Defining Moment in Global AI Governance

Artificial intelligence is reshaping the world at an extraordinary pace, influencing economies, societies, and industries. As AI development accelerates, nations and organizations face an urgent challenge: ensuring that AI is ethical, sustainable, and inclusive. Addressing this challenge, over 100 countries convened in Paris on February 10-11, 2025, for the AI Action Summit, co-hosted by France and India.

This landmark event aimed to move beyond AI risk discussions and focus on actionable strategies that will shape AI as a force for public good, bridging the global digital divide, fostering innovation, and ensuring AI governance aligns with ethical standards.

Redefining AI Governance: A Shift Toward Action

Unlike previous AI summits that centred primarily on safety and risk management, this summit marked a shift toward practical implementation and inclusive global AI strategies. The discussions were built around three core principles:

  • Scientific Collaboration – Ensuring AI development is guided by rigorous research and aligned with international best practices.
  • Solution-Oriented Approaches – Emphasizing open AI models that comply with national policies while fostering transparency and inclusivity.
  • Global Policy Standards – Establishing governance structures that promote AI safety, accessibility, and sustainability.

French President Emmanuel Macron underscored AI’s transformational impact by presenting AI-generated deepfake videos of himself, demonstrating both the potential and challenges AI presents. He highlighted AI’s profound role in healthcare, energy, and economic development, stressing:

Using artificial intelligence, we can do some very big things: change healthcare, energy, life in our society.

His remarks set the tone for discussions on how AI should be harnessed responsibly to drive progress while addressing global disparities.

Key Priorities and Commitments from the AI Action Summit

The summit reinforced the importance of inclusive AI policies by building upon established global frameworks, including the UNESCO Recommendation on AI Ethics, the Global Digital Compact, the African Union’s AI Strategy, and the G7 Hiroshima AI Process. The discussions led to the identification of six major priorities:

  • Expanding AI Accessibility – Bridging the global digital divide to ensure AI benefits are equitably distributed across nations, preventing a technological gap that favours only developed economies.
  • Ensuring Ethical and Transparent AI – Strengthening AI governance to ensure models are transparent, safe, and aligned with international human rights frameworks.
  • Fostering AI Innovation Without Market Monopolization – Creating an environment where AI thrives as an open, competitive, and accessible technology, preventing the dominance of a few corporations or nations.
  • AI and the Future of Work – Addressing how AI is reshaping labor markets and ensuring policies support skill development, job security, and equitable employment opportunities.
  • Sustainable AI Development – Investing in energy-efficient AI systems and reducing the environmental impact of AI infrastructure to ensure AI growth aligns with climate sustainability goals.
  • Strengthening International AI Cooperation – Enhancing multilateral partnerships to harmonize AI governance strategies across nations while avoiding fragmentation or contradictory regulations.

The discussions emphasized that while AI offers vast potential, it must be managed in a way that promotes inclusivity, protects human rights, and prevents economic disparities.

Concrete Actions: The Launch of the Public Interest AI Platform

One of the most significant outcomes of the summit was the launch of the Public Interest AI Platform and Incubator, an initiative designed to:

  • Support AI-driven public goods such as education, healthcare, and digital infrastructure.
  • Enhance transparency in AI development, ensuring fairness, accountability, and bias reduction.
  • Build AI capacity in developing nations, offering resources for technical training, computing power, and funding.

Additionally, for the first time, the summit brought AI and energy efficiency into the global discussion, recognizing that AI’s rapid expansion has raised concerns about its environmental footprint. The summit resulted in key commitments to:

  • Invest in sustainable AI infrastructure, including energy-efficient cloud computing and green AI technologies.
  • Create an international observatory, in partnership with the International Energy Agency, to monitor AI’s energy consumption and environmental impact.
  • Encourage AI innovation that prioritizes sustainability, showcasing solutions that reduce power consumption while maintaining high-performance AI capabilities.

The AI Action Summit also highlighted the growing impact of AI on employment and called for the establishment of a global observatory network to study AI’s influence on job markets, workforce training, and fair labour practices.

Strengthening Global AI Governance: Aligning Policies and Regulations

A key takeaway from the summit was the recognition that effective AI governance requires global cooperation. Delegates reaffirmed commitments to:

  • Ensuring AI development respects international laws, including human rights protections, gender equality, and linguistic diversity.
  • Strengthening intellectual property protections, particularly in the wake of AI-generated content and deepfake technology.
  • Continuing discussions on AI safety were initiated by previous global summits, such as the Bletchley Park AI Safety Summit and the Seoul AI Summit.

Notably, while 58 countries endorsed the AI declaration, major AI players like the United States and the United Kingdom declined to sign, citing concerns over governance frameworks and regulatory clarity. Their absence raises critical questions:

  • Can AI governance succeed without universal participation from major AI powers?
  • Will nations develop independent AI strategies, leading to fragmented policies?
  • How will countries balance AI regulation with the need to remain competitive in the global AI race?

The Road Ahead: Future AI Summits & Policy Milestones

The AI Action Summit was only the beginning of a broader global effort to establish AI governance structures. Key upcoming events will continue shaping AI’s regulatory and ethical future:

  • Kigali Summit 2025 – Addressing AI’s role in Africa’s economic and technological growth.
  • 3rd Global Forum on the Ethics of AI (Thailand & UNESCO) – Refining international AI ethics guidelines.
  • World AI Conference 2025 – Exploring AI’s implications on global economies and industries.
  • AI for Good Global Summit 2025 – Showcasing AI’s potential in humanitarian and social impact projects.
